### 💡 Tips that keep holding up

- **AI proofreads, never ghostwrites.** Let the model tighten the work, not replace the human voice — it keeps output trustworthy and yours.
- **Prototype to prove *or kill*.** Build a scrappy proof-of-concept to validate (or retire) a use case *before* engineering commits. Killing bad ideas early is the cheapest thing you'll ever do.
- **Separation-of-duties for agents; a human as the sole merge gate.** Architect, builder, and reviewer agents each own one job, and nothing ships without a person approving the PR.
- **Decisions can't live only in chat.** Write them down — ADRs, a glossary, a spine. It's what lets a solo builder move at small-team velocity without drift.
